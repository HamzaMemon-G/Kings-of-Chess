Rules of Chess

  1. The chessboard is a square grid of 64 squares, alternating in color between light and dark.
  2. The game is played between two opponents, each starting with 16 pieces of different types.
  3. The objective of the game is to checkmate your opponent's king by putting it in a position
    where it is under attack and cannot escape capture.
  4. Each piece moves in a unique way, and capturing an opponent's piece involves moving your
    own piece to the square occupied by the opponent's piece.
  5. Players take turns moving their pieces, and a game can end in a win, loss, or draw.
